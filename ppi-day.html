The Day
How it unfolded
Property closing, three PPIs, and a truck decision — all on April 7, 2026.
Morning
912 Westwood Ranch — Closed
40.22 acres in Ash Fork, AZ. $50,000. The land is ours.
8:40 PM — RO#10251
Brue (Tundra) — PASSED
5 minor findings. Brakes needed (pulsating rotors), TPMS light, paint fade. Everything else clean. Brand new radiator just installed.
“The Toyota is a winner. I literally couldn’t find anything wrong besides needing brake pads and rotors all around. No leaks and it drives good.” — Rick
9:30 PM — RO#10252
Beto (2500HD) — ELIMINATED
9 concerns. Hydroboost leaking (brake assist), valve cover gaskets leaking, control arm bushings cracked, ball joints shot, front axle boots torn, A/C weak. $1,700-4,050 in repairs on top of purchase. The “rebuild” didn’t touch the chassis.
Rick said it’s a solid truck that just needs attention — the big tires didn’t do it any favors and it rides rough.
10:33 PM — RO#10253
Julian (Sierra) — ELIMINATED
7 concerns. The 4L60E transmission flagged sluggish with delayed shifting — exactly the failure mode we asked Rick to check. Control arm bushings dry rotted (same as Beto). Oil pan leaking. EVAP large leak. A/C weak. $2,300-4,300 in repairs.
The golden era dream at $5K — but Rick found the trans is on borrowed time. Can’t build a property truck on a transmission that’s already slipping.
Decision
Brue wins. Call Kyle.
The only truck that passed clean. 8.35 vs 6.95 vs 6.55. Not even close. $11,500 to a fair seller for a proven truck.

Beyond the Numbers
What each truck uniquely brings
Brue (Tundra) — Reliability
Toyota’s flagship V8. 6-speed auto that won’t need a rebuild. Fully boxed frame. Clean PPI with only brakes. New radiator. Seller cited KBB unprompted. Finding a Tundra in this shape with a straightforward seller — that doesn’t happen twice.
Julian (Sierra) — Value (Eliminated)
The golden era spec at half the price. 5.3L Vortec, no AFM, iron block, crew cab, 4WD. Would have saved $7,300 over 5 years. But Rick found the 4L60E trans sluggish with delayed shifts — a $1,500-2,000 rebuild waiting to happen. Control arms rotted. Oil pan leaking. The math only works if the truck is sound, and this one isn’t.
Beto (2500HD) — Capability (Eliminated)
3/4 ton, 2,000 lb payload, 4L80E (indestructible trans), fully boxed HD frame. Could haul gravel, lumber, and the Cedarshed. But 268K miles of suspension wear, hydroboost leak, and $1,700-4,050 in repairs killed the value proposition.
